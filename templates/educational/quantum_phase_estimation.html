Quantum Phase Estimation

Overview

Quantum Phase Estimation (QPE) is a quantum algorithm that estimates the phase of an eigenvalue of a unitary operator. It is a fundamental subroutine in many quantum algorithms, including Shor's algorithm.

Key Concepts

  • Eigenvalue: A scalar value associated with an eigenvector of a linear operator
  • Phase: The angle in the complex plane of an eigenvalue
  • Inverse Quantum Fourier Transform: Used to extract the phase information

Algorithm Steps

  1. Prepare a superposition state
  2. Apply controlled unitary operations
  3. Apply inverse Quantum Fourier Transform
  4. Measure the output register

Applications

  • Shor's algorithm for integer factorization
  • Quantum chemistry simulations
  • Solving linear systems of equations
  • Quantum machine learning

Limitations

  • Precision limited by the number of qubits
  • Requires controlled unitary operations
  • Sensitive to quantum noise and decoherence
  • Computational complexity depends on the precision required
