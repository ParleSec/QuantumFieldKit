Quantum Fourier Transform

Overview

The Quantum Fourier Transform (QFT) is a quantum analogue of the classical discrete Fourier transform. It is a fundamental building block for many quantum algorithms, including Shor's algorithm for factoring.

Key Concepts

  • Quantum States: Represented as vectors in a complex Hilbert space
  • Phase Estimation: Determining the phase of an eigenvalue of a unitary operator
  • Quantum Gates: Hadamard and controlled phase gates used to implement QFT

Algorithm Steps

  1. Apply Hadamard gates to all qubits
  2. Apply controlled phase gates between qubits
  3. Reverse the order of qubits

Applications

  • Shor's algorithm for integer factorization
  • Quantum phase estimation
  • Quantum signal processing
  • Solving linear systems of equations

Limitations

  • Requires precise control of quantum gates
  • Sensitive to quantum noise and decoherence
  • Limited by the number of qubits available
