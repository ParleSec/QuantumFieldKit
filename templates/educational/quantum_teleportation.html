Quantum Teleportation

Overview

Quantum teleportation is a process by which quantum information can be transmitted from one location to another, using a classical communication channel and quantum entanglement. It does not involve the physical transfer of matter.

Key Concepts

  • Quantum Entanglement: A special connection between quantum particles
  • Bell State: A maximally entangled quantum state of two qubits
  • Classical Communication: Required to complete the teleportation process

Protocol Steps

  1. Create an entangled pair of qubits (Bell state)
  2. Perform a Bell measurement on the qubit to be teleported and one of the entangled pair
  3. Transmit the measurement results classically
  4. Apply appropriate quantum gates based on the measurement results

Applications

  • Quantum communication networks
  • Quantum computing
  • Quantum cryptography
  • Distributed quantum computing

Limitations

  • Requires classical communication
  • Original quantum state is destroyed during teleportation
  • Requires pre-shared entanglement
  • Sensitive to quantum noise and decoherence
