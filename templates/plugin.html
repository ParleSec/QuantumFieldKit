Quantum Explanation
{% if plugin.key == 'bb84' %}
BB84 Quantum Key Distribution Protocol

BB84 is the first quantum cryptography protocol, developed by Charles Bennett and Gilles Brassard in 1984. It allows two parties (traditionally called Alice and Bob) to establish a shared random secret key using quantum communication.

Key aspects:

  • Quantum States: Information is encoded in quantum states (qubits) using two different bases.
  • No-Cloning Theorem: Quantum mechanics prevents perfect copying of unknown quantum states.
  • Eavesdropper Detection: Any measurement by an eavesdropper will disturb the quantum states, revealing their presence.
{% elif plugin.key == 'teleport' %}
Quantum Teleportation

Quantum teleportation is a process by which quantum information can be transmitted from one location to another, with the help of classical communication and previously shared quantum entanglement.

Key aspects:

  • Entanglement: Relies on pre-shared entangled qubits between sender and receiver.
  • Bell Measurement: The sender performs a joint measurement on their qubit and one half of the entangled pair.
  • Classical Communication: Measurement results are sent via classical channels.
  • Quantum Reconstruction: The receiver applies corrections based on the classical information.
{% elif plugin.key == 'grover' %}
Grover's Search Algorithm

Grover's algorithm provides a quadratic speedup for unstructured search problems. It can find a marked item in a database of N items with approximately √N queries, compared to the classical O(N) requirement.

Key aspects:

  • Oracle Function: Marks the target state by flipping its phase.
  • Amplitude Amplification: Iteratively increases the probability of measuring the target state.
  • Quantum Interference: Uses constructive and destructive interference to amplify the correct answer.

How It Works

This simulation uses Google Cirq, a Python framework for creating, editing, and invoking Noisy Intermediate Scale Quantum (NISQ) circuits. The backend performs these steps:

  1. Constructs quantum circuit based on your parameters
  2. Applies specified quantum gates and operations
  3. Simulates quantum behavior with high fidelity
  4. Performs measurements and collects results
  5. Renders visualizations of the quantum behavior
